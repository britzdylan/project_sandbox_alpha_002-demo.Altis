# ODA Squad Management — Player Guide

---

## Your Team

You command a 12-man Operational Detachment Alpha — a Special Forces A-Team. These are not generic soldiers. Each member has a Military Occupational Specialty (MOS) that defines their role, their available gear options, and the passive advantages they bring to the operation.

**Your ODA:**

| Role                 | MOS  | Slots | What they bring                          |
|----------------------|------|-------|------------------------------------------|
| Scout                | 18X  | 2     | Recon, flexibility, widest gear options  |
| Weapons Sergeant     | 18B  | 2     | Fire support, anti-tank, heavy weapons   |
| Engineer             | 18C  | 2     | Demolitions, obstacle clearance          |
| Intelligence Officer | 18F  | 2     | UAV access, ISR support                  |
| Communications SGT   | 18E  | 2     | Comms systems, support call advantages   |
| Medic                | 18D  | 1     | Keeps the team alive                     |

You start with all 11 slots available. You choose who deploys, who stays back, and what they carry.

---

## The Squad Manager

Your primary tool for managing the team is the **Squad Manager** at the TOC (Tactical Operations Center).

**To open it:** Walk up to the Squad Manager board and hold the interact key.

The Squad Manager shows your full roster — every slot, their current status, and their loadout. From here you can:

- **Deploy** a standby soldier into the field
- **Dismiss** a deployed soldier back to standby
- **Change a soldier's loadout** before or during a mission

### Status Colors

At a glance, the status column tells you where each soldier stands:

| Color  | Status   | Meaning                                              |
|--------|----------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Green  | ACTIVE   | Deployed and in the field with you                   |
| Gray   | STANDBY  | At base, ready to deploy                             |
| Orange | INCAP    | Down in the field — needs medical attention          |
| Yellow | R&R      | Temporarily unavailable                              |
| Red    | KIA      | Dead — replacement en route                         |
| Blue   | INBOUND  | Replacement inbound, not yet arrived                 |

---

## Deploying and Dismissing

**To deploy a soldier:**
1. Open the Squad Manager
2. Select a STANDBY slot
3. Click **Deploy** — they will appear at your position in the field

**To dismiss a soldier:**
1. Open the Squad Manager
2. Select an ACTIVE slot
3. Click **Dismiss** — they stand down and return to STANDBY

Dismissed soldiers are unharmed and available again immediately. Use dismissal to rotate your team, adjust composition before a mission, or send someone back when you need a smaller element.

> You can deploy and dismiss at any time the TOC is accessible, including mid-mission if you return to base.

---

## Loadouts

Each soldier's available gear options are tied to their MOS. A Medic carries different configurations than a Weapons Sergeant.

**To change a loadout:**
1. Select a soldier in the Squad Manager (ACTIVE or STANDBY)
2. Use the **Loadout** dropdown to pick a configuration
3. The change applies immediately — if they're in the field, they re-equip on the spot

Loadout families follow a naming pattern: **FACTION × ROLE**

- **NATO** — standard issue, high visibility
- **GUERRILLA** — local-pattern gear, lighter footprint
- **STEALTH** — suppressed weapons, low-profile kit

Choose your loadout based on the mission. A direct assault calls for different gear than a long-range recon patrol.

---

## When a Soldier Goes Down

Your soldiers are not invincible. When a soldier takes enough damage to be lethal, they go **incapacitated** rather than dying outright. They drop to the ground and cannot fight.

**You have a window to save them.** If nobody reaches them in time, they die.

---

### Reviving a Soldier Yourself

**Requirements:**
- Be within 5 meters of the downed soldier
- Have a **Medkit** in your inventory

**To revive:**
- Hold the interact key while near the downed soldier
- Maintain the hold for the full revive duration
- The soldier will recover with reduced health

> A Medkit is not the same as a Medikit or First Aid Kit. Check your inventory before heading out.

---

### Medic Auto-Revive

If your **Medic (18D)** is in the field and a soldier goes down, the Medic will automatically attempt to revive them — no player input required.

**The Medic will respond if:**
- They are alive and not incapacitated themselves
- They have a **Medikit** in their inventory
- They are within **25 meters** of the downed soldier

When triggered, the Medic will move to the casualty, perform the treatment, and get them back up. The Medic is faster than a player revive.

**Keeping your Medic alive and equipped is one of the highest-value decisions you can make.**

---

### The Timer

Once a soldier is incapacitated, a hidden countdown begins. If nobody revives them before the timer runs out, they die permanently.

You will not see a timer on screen — the urgency is real but undisplayed. Prioritize casualties immediately. A soldier left alone in a firefight will not survive.

---

## Permanent Death and Replacement

If a soldier dies — either because the revive window expired or they took catastrophic damage — they are gone.

Their slot will show **KIA** in the Squad Manager.

A replacement soldier from outside the AO will be dispatched. This takes **time** — real elapsed mission time, not just sitting at the menu. The replacement arrives automatically when the timer expires; their slot returns to STANDBY and they can be deployed like any other soldier.

> Permanent losses matter. You cannot pause the replacement clock, and you cannot rush it. Protect your team.

---

## Practical Tips

**Take the Medic.** The 18D slot provides automatic casualty response that no other soldier can replicate. Losing your Medic means every future incapacitation depends entirely on your position and your Medkit supply.

**Stage your team at the TOC before you move out.** Deploy the composition you want, set loadouts, and confirm everyone is equipped before you leave base. Changing gear mid-operation means backtracking.

**Match loadouts to the mission.** If you're going in quietly, put your scouts in stealth kit and suppress your weapons. If you're expecting a hard fight, bring the heavier NATO configurations.

**Don't overextend your squad.** You have 11 slots but you don't need all 11 in the field at once. A smaller, well-coordinated element is harder to attrite than a large spread-out one. Keep some in standby as a reserve.

**React to incapacitations immediately.** If a soldier goes down in contact, break contact or suppress — then get to them. A soldier who dies in the field becomes a 6-hour problem.

**Keep your Medic's Medikit stocked.** The auto-revive system checks for a Medikit in inventory. If the Medic expends their kit and you don't resupply them, they lose the ability to revive casualties automatically.
